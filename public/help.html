Getting started

Getting started with Theory Maker Drag & Drop

Be careful, still in beta! Only works in Chrome, Firefox or Opera. Also try theorymaker.info, the text-based alternative.

  • Zoom in: Scroll with mouse wheel (or two finger drag on your trackpad)
  • Move diagram around: Click on the background and drag
  • Add a new Variable: Double-click on the diagram OR drag away from one Variable, using the ➦ icon.
  • Change or delete something: Click the ✎ icon (near the end of an Arrow, at the top-left of Variables and Groups) OR right-click on it. To finish, just click the diagram background.
  • Add an arrow: Hover your mouse over a Variable or Group and click on the ➦ icon at the top-right, then drag to another or to an empty space (press Ctrl+Shift at the same time to get a reverse Arrow).
  • Add a Group box: Click on the Variables you want, right-click, “Add group”.
  • Move a Variable or a Group: Drag it and drop it.
  • Move a Variable into and out of Groups: Near the top-right corner of the Variable, grab the ➟ icon.

Using the right-hand panel (if you can’t see it, click on the gear-wheel icon)

  • Fold and unfold sections of the panel: click on the
  • Save your diagram online: In the Save section, type a name and press “Save diagram online”. You can revisit it again at the link which appears. An online png image is also created.
  • Download your diagram as a picture: Zoom in or out as required, Select the type you want ( emf is good for Microsoft Office docs), Click download.
  • Copy your diagram to the clipboard: click “Create Web Graphic”, then Ctrl+C (or right-click / copy), then click to return to app.
  • Change the appearance of all your Variables, Groups and/or Arrows: change the settings in the right-hand panel.
Extra tips

Extra tips

  • If something is not working - save the diagram online to “refresh” it.
  • Select more than one item - click on each one.
  • De-select all items - click on the background.
  • Change appearance of more than one item - select several items then right-click or press ⚙.
  • To arrange Variables in a column with even spacing - click the ⚙ and check “Fixed vertical spacing”. The Variables are now marked with a ↨.
  • Stop the arrows “flowing” - in the right-hand panel, section “Arrow”, unclick “Flow arrow”.
  • Get a better quality graphic download - in the right-hand panel, section “Save”, change the slider (only necessary for png graphics).
  • Save your diagram on your desktop - in the right-hand panel, section “Save”, click “Save to desktop”. Later, re-load the same file using Load from desktop.
  • Apply themes - in the right-hand panel, section “Diagram”, select a theme. De-select “change existing items” if you only want your theme to apply to new items.
  • Add icons When editing text, click on an icon in the palette below. Or copy and paste from Emojipedia. You can also use character codes like &phone;, which produces ☎
  • To create several Variables at once - when editing one Variable label, type or paste your text with double line-breaks where you want new Variables. When you finish, corresponding Variables are created underneath.
  • Add notes to something - right-click on it and type something in the “Notes” box. The notes also appear as a tool-tip (when you hover over the item).
  • Curve an Arrow - right-click on it or click the ⚙ and type a number in the Curved % box. Negative numbers e.g. -22 curve the other way.
  • Fix the start or end of an Arrow to a particular corner - right-click on it or click the ⚙ then change the Source or Target.

Keyboard shortcuts:

  • Delete on one or more selected item(s): delete the item(s).
  • Ctrl+z undo previous change(s). Press several times if necessary.
  • Enter on a selected item: edit the label. To finish, press Ctrl+Enter.
  • Arrow keys: navigate around connected Variables (left: move to parent; right: move to child; up/down: move to siblings).
  • Ctrl+Left-arrow: create a new Variable to the left of the selected Variable.
  • Ctrl+Right-arrow: create a new Variable to the right of the selected Variable.
  • Ctrl+Down-arrow (used on a Variable which is parent of another Variable): create another parent.
  • Ctrl+Up-arrow (used on a Variable which is child of another Variable): create another child.
